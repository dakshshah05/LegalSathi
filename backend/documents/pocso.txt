The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012
This Act was enacted by the Parliament of India to protect children (any person under 18 years of age) from offenses of sexual assault, sexual harassment, pornography, and exploitation.

Key Rights and Protective Safeguards under POCSO:
1. Definition of Child: Every individual below 18 years of age is a child under this law, regardless of gender or consent.
2. Mandatory Reporting: Under Section 19, any person (including school staff, doctors, neighbors, or family members) who suspects or knows of child sexual abuse must report it. Failure to report is a punishable crime.
3. Victim Anonymity: Section 23 strictly prohibits the media, courts, or any public report from disclosing the child's identity, name, school, address, family members, or photograph. Disclosing the victim's identity is punishable by up to 1 year of imprisonment.
4. Non-Bailable Arrests: All major offenses under the POCSO Act, such as penetrative sexual assault or aggravated sexual assault, are cognisable and non-bailable. The accused remains in custody during investigations.
5. Child-Friendly Procedures: The child's statement is recorded at their residence or preferred location by a police officer in plain clothes. The child must not be kept in a police lockup or court jail under any circumstances.
6. Special Court Environment: POCSO cases are tried in Special Courts. The judge does not sit in uniform. The court must ensure that the child does not come face-to-face with the accused during testimonies (using screens, curtains, or video link).
